Seed germination

Published on: 01 Jan 2024 | Author: John Doe

General Information on Seed Germination:
1.Seed Coat: The outer protective layer of the seed. It is often shed when the seed begins to germinate.
2.Radicle (Root): The first part of the seedling to emerge from the seed, growing downward to form the root system.
3.Cotyledons (Seed Leaves): The initial leaves produced by the seed. They provide nutrients to the young plant during the early stages of development.
4.Shoot (Plumule): The part of the seedling that grows upward, eventually developing into the plant's stem and leaves.

Phases of Germination:
1.Imbibition: The seed absorbs water, causing it to swell.
2.Radicle Emergence: The radicle breaks through the seed coat and begins to grow downwards.
3.Shoot Development: The shoot starts to grow upwards toward the light.
4.True Leaves Formation: After the cotyledons, true leaves form, allowing the plant to begin photosynthesis.
